# Leslie Groff: The Scheduler Who Was Never Indicted

*Every quote is extracted verbatim from OCR text of EFTA documents. Every EFTA page number is verified against the original PDF. Cross-references to [rhowardstone/Epstein-research](https://github.com/rhowardstone/Epstein-research) are credited where used.*

## Summary

Leslie Groff worked as Epstein's primary New York-based assistant from at least 2001 through the summer of 2019 — eighteen years spanning the entire period of known abuse. She is named by virtually every victim in the SDNY prosecution memo as the person who scheduled their massages. SDNY gave her a reverse proffer two weeks after Epstein's arrest. Her attorney refused to bring her in and advised her to invoke the Fifth Amendment. She was never indicted.

This document assembles every Groff reference from the VOL00012 prosecution memos with cross-volume data from rhowardstone's analysis to reconstruct what SDNY knew and what they planned to do about it.

## What the Memo Says About Her Role

SDNY describes Groff's role in a section titled "Counsel for Leslie Groff":

> "Leslie Groff worked as Epstein's primary New York-based assistant from at least in or about 2001 through the summer of 2019. Her responsibilities included, among other things, scheduling Epstein's massages at the New York residence. Generally, Groff would call victims to schedule massage appointments, but she had limited interactions with the victims beyond scheduling. In addition to scheduling massage appointments, Groff would also book flights for girls who travelled to meet Epstein at his various residences. Groff made these arrangements by phone from the New York office where she worked for Epstein. She spent little time in Epstein's residence and thus had few in-person interactions with victims." (Source: EFTA02731146)

This characterization — "limited interactions," "spent little time in Epstein's residence" — is SDNY's summary of Groff's role. It should be read alongside what victims say.

## What Victims Say About Her

Groff appears in the testimony of virtually every victim. Across the 86-page prosecution memo, she is mentioned 50 times.

**Victim-1** (NYC recruiter, age 14):
> "Victim-1 would schedule appointments for the girls by phone with Epstein's assistant, 'Leslie.' Based on the investigation, we have identified 'Leslie' as Leslie Groff." (Source: EFTA02731041)

**Victim-3** (Florida, phone record corroboration):
> "Victim-3 generally scheduled the massages through [redacted]. Victim-3 recalled that [redacted] would sometimes call from New York to schedule massages in advance of Epstein's arrival in Florida" (Source: EFTA02731045)

SDNY verified this with records: calls between [redacted] and Victim-3 correlated with Epstein's flight schedule (e.g., September 16, 2004: calls in the afternoon, then a JFK→West Palm Beach flight that night). (Source: EFTA02731046)

**Multiple adult victims** interviewed in August 2019:
> "[Victim] recalled that Leslie Groff booked [her travel]" (Source: EFTA02731085)

> "Leslie Groff would contact [her] to [schedule massages]... Groff did not travel with Epstein." (Source: EFTA02731089)

> "[Victim] recalled that Leslie Groff and Maxwell coordinated the travel logistics for her and Epstein. She recalls that Groff was right outside the closed door to Epstein's office on one [occasion]... [Victim questioned] whether Groff knew what was happening inside the office." (Source: EFTA02731108)

> "[Victim] recalled that Leslie Groff would schedule her massage appointments with Epstein and make her travel arrangements. Groff worked in an office inside Epstein's New York [residence]." (Source: EFTA02731110)

**The victim who says Groff "ruined her life":**
> "[Victim] generally [reported] that Epstein, Maxwell, [redacted], and Groff all ruined her life, but she was too distressed to answer questions about what each of those individuals did." (Source: EFTA02731117)

This victim places Groff in the same category as Epstein and Maxwell — not as a mere scheduler.

## The December 2018 Email and the $250K Wire

Six days after the Miami Herald published its investigative series on Epstein (November 28, 2018), Groff emailed a former victim:

> "in early December 2018, Leslie Groff emailed [redacted] inviting her to visit with Epstein at his residence in New York." (Source: EFTA02731133)

The victim accepted the invitation:

> "[Redacted] accepted the invitation and once again told Epstein about her financial difficulties. In response, Epstein made a call to his accountant, Rich Kahn, and directed Kahn to wire $250,000 to [redacted]. Epstein [told redacted] not to tell anyone about the payment" (Source: EFTA02731133)

The victim mentioned this payment to another former associate:

> "[Redacted] mentioned the payment she received to [redacted], who responded that she had recently received a payment of $100,000 from Epstein." (Source: EFTA02731134)

When asked about the timing:

> "[Redacted] recalls that she received the email from Groff inviting her to meet with Epstein within a day or two of the [Miami Herald] series' publication." (Source: EFTA02731134)

> "she does not recall the details of what they discussed and did not believe the payment and articles were related." (Source: EFTA02731134)

**Context from rhowardstone:** The $250K wire was cited by the government in Epstein's detention memo alongside a separate $100K wire to Nadia Marcinkova's Aviloop LLC (November 30, 2018) and a $250K wire to a redacted co-conspirator (December 3, 2018) as evidence of "post-Herald emergency payments." (Source: rhowardstone/Epstein-research, citing EFTA00028785, DS8. Credit: rhowardstone/Epstein-research.)

Groff's role was sending the invitation email. The wire was directed by Epstein through Kahn. But the timing — days after the Herald series — is what the government used to argue witness tampering.

## The Reverse Proffer and Fifth Amendment

Two weeks after Epstein's arrest:

> "On July 18, 2019, we gave a reverse proffer to Leslie Groff, during which we explained that numerous victims had indicated that she was responsible for scheduling massages during which they were sexually abused. We encouraged Groff to meet with us for a proffer so that she could share her story and explain her role." (Source: EFTA02731146)

Three weeks later, her attorney responded:

> "on August 7, 2019, we had a phone call with Groff's attorney during which he indicated that he would not bring Groff in for a proffer and had advised her to invoke her Fifth Amendment privilege against self-incrimination if she is subpoenaed to testify." (Source: EFTA02731146)

SDNY pushed back:

> "In recent weeks, we have reached back out to Groff's attorney to convey that we will not be able to provide her with any sort of agreement if she does not proffer with us. He appeared to understand our position and is considering whether to bring Groff in for a proffer." (Source: EFTA02731146)

As of the memo's writing, the outcome was unresolved. Groff was never indicted.

## The Charging Discussion

Groff was explicitly named in the memo's discussion of potential charges:

> "[This memo details the steps taken in this investigation since July 2019, summarizes the potential charges for the conduct at issue, and analyzes the evidence against certain of Epstein's associates and employees:] [redacted], Leslie Groff, and Ghislaine Maxwell." (Source: EFTA02731084)

A later section of the memo is titled "D. Leslie Groff" and appears on EFTA02731167, immediately before the Maxwell prosecution memorandum begins on EFTA02731168. The OCR captured only the heading — the content beneath is either redacted or not captured.

## What the NPA Did Not Cover

The 2007 Non-Prosecution Agreement named specific co-conspirators who were protected: Kellen, Marcinkova, and Ross. Groff was not named:

> "[redacted], Lesley Groff, or [redacted], none of whom were signatories to the NPA." (Source: EFTA02731068)

This means Groff had no legal immunity from the NPA. She could have been charged at any point.

## Full-Text Corpus Evidence

Querying rhowardstone's full-text corpus database (2.89M pages, 1.4M unique EFTA numbers) revealed the scale of Groff's presence and provided direct email evidence of her operational role.

**Scale:** Groff's name appears across an extraordinarily large number of corpus pages, concentrated in the email datasets (DS9, DS10, DS11). This reflects her central role as Epstein's communications hub — virtually every scheduling email, travel arrangement, and contact coordination passed through her.

**Direct massage scheduling emails** — These are the most significant corpus finds because they show Groff actively arranging massages by email, contradicting the "limited interactions" characterization:

- **EFTA01940212 (DS10, January 2014):** Groff emails a woman: "Jeffrey is back in PB and asking if you might be available for a massage later this afternoon." This is Groff directly soliciting someone for a massage on Epstein's behalf in Palm Beach.

- **EFTA01941335 (DS10, January 2014):** Epstein emails Groff: "call [redacted] see if she has time to do massage this afternoon. thanks" — the chain of command documented in a single email.

- **EFTA02043921 (DS10, September 2016):** Groff forwards "Thai Massage Sacred Bodywork Upcoming Workshops" to Epstein. Eight years after his conviction, Groff is still forwarding massage-related content to Epstein.

- **EFTA02206177 (DS11, February 2017):** Groff emails Deepak Chopra's team: "...massages?...Jeffrey will be in NY the week of March 13th....thank you!! Lesley" — scheduling massages through a celebrity contact, 2017.

**Top connections** (names co-occurring with Groff on corpus pages): Richard Kahn (highest), Bella Klein, Darren Indyke, Larry Visoski, Ghislaine Maxwell, Deepak Chopra, Eva Dubin, Jean-Luc Brunel, Senator George Mitchell.

## The Contradiction

The memo characterizes Groff as having "limited interactions" and "few in-person interactions with victims." But the evidence in the same memo shows:

1. She was named by virtually every victim as their scheduling contact
2. She scheduled massages she knew were sexual in nature (the reverse proffer explicitly told her "numerous victims had indicated that she was responsible for scheduling massages during which they were sexually abused")
3. She booked flights for underage girls to travel to Epstein
4. She was "right outside the closed door" during at least one abuse session
5. One victim equates her with Maxwell and Epstein as people who "ruined her life"
6. She emailed a former victim days after the Herald series, leading to a $250K payment the government cited as potential witness tampering
7. She was active through May 2019 — six weeks before arrest
8. Her attorney invoked the Fifth Amendment — which implies awareness of criminal liability
9. SDNY explicitly analyzed potential charges against her in a dedicated section
10. The full-text corpus shows her directly scheduling massages by email — "Jeffrey is back in PB and asking if you might be available for a massage this afternoon" (EFTA01940212)
11. She forwarded massage workshop information to Epstein as late as September 2016 (EFTA02043921)
12. She coordinated massages through Deepak Chopra's team in February 2017 (EFTA02206177)

The "limited interactions" language appears designed to explain why Groff was not charged, despite the evidence suggesting she was far more than a passive scheduler. The corpus email evidence is particularly damaging to this characterization — it shows Groff proactively soliciting women for massages on Epstein's behalf, not merely answering phone calls.

## Limitations

1. **Section "D. Leslie Groff" (EFTA02731167) is missing from the release.** Both our OCR and the rhowardstone full-text corpus (which uses a different extraction pipeline) show only the section heading — 179 characters. The substantive charging analysis for Groff was never in the released PDF. This is a gap in the EFTA release itself, not an extraction failure. It is the single most important missing piece.

2. **The proffer outcome is unknown.** The memo ends with Groff's attorney "considering" whether to bring her in. Whether she ever proffered, and if so what she said, is not in the public record.

3. **The "limited interactions" claim is SDNY's own characterization.** We cannot independently verify how much time Groff spent in Epstein's residence. The characterization may be accurate, or it may reflect a prosecutorial decision to deprioritize her.

4. **The Kellen plea (DS9) shows a comparator.** Sarah Kellen, who performed a similar scheduling role, ultimately pled guilty. Groff did not. The NPA protected Kellen but not Groff — yet Groff was never charged while Kellen eventually was. The reason for this asymmetry is not documented.

5. **This analysis covers VOL00012, rhowardstone's reports, and the full-text corpus.** The corpus search provides comprehensive coverage, but the email evidence has not been read in full — only snippets from database queries. A full reading of all Groff emails could reveal additional operational details.
