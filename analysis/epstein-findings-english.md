## 3. Epstein's Payment Pattern — Systematic Silence Buying

**Source:** EFTA02731127–EFTA02731133 (epstein_ren16.txt, VOL00012)

The memo documents a pattern where Epstein paid victims in connection with negative media coverage:

- Epstein covered medical treatment and attorney costs when a victim was deposed in 2015
- "Whenever negative articles were published, Epstein would wire her money"
- $100,000 wired shortly after the Miami Herald's coverage
- In December 2018 — six months before the arrest — Leslie Groff emailed the victim inviting her to NYC. Epstein called his accountant Rich Kahn and instructed him to wire $250,000 to the victim
- Epstein told her not to tell anyone about the payment
- Previous payment was $100,000 as a wedding gift

**Pattern:** The payments correlate with legal and media risk, not care. The $250,000 wire six months before arrest, combined with instructions for secrecy, suggests active silence buying during a period when Epstein knew investigation was approaching.

**Source:** EFTA00009220–00009221 (epstein_ren13.txt, VOL00007) — OPR interview of R. Alexander Acosta, October 18, 2019

Acosta was asked directly about the press conference claim that he had been told Epstein was "an intelligence asset of some sort." His response:

**Context:** Acosta was also "firm on two years" of prison time but ended at 18 months. He admitted the outcome would have been different in a purely federal case. He applied "petite policy" — assessing whether the state sentence was so unreasonable that federal intervention was necessary, not what constituted adequate punishment.

The memo describes a complete control system over at least one long-term victim:

- **Physical control:** Epstein dictated the victim's hair color (short, blonde), chose her clothes, commented on her body, and pressured her to lose weight

**The Maxwell birthday ritual:** On Epstein's birthday (circa 2002), Maxwell instructed the victim and other young women to undress, wrap themselves in plastic wrap, pose around Epstein as he sat on a massage table, and press paint on their breasts onto a canvas as a gift.

The memo documents systematic evidence annihilation:
