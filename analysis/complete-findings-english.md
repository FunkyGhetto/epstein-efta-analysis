# What the Epstein Files Actually Say: A Systematic Analysis of 2.89 Million Pages

*Every claim is sourced to specific EFTA document numbers from the public release at [justice.gov/epstein](https://www.justice.gov/epstein). All 56 EFTA citations were verified against rhowardstone's independent full-text corpus (2,892,730 pages). Cross-referenced against [rhowardstone/Epstein-research](https://github.com/rhowardstone/Epstein-research) (165+ reports). Internet-verified March 22, 2026.*

---

## 1. What We Did

One person directed two AI tools to process 4,251 documents released under the Epstein Files Transparency Act (H.R. 4405). The OCR text was searched, the key passages were extracted, every citation was verified against original PDFs, and the results were cross-referenced against a 2.89-million-page corpus database and the largest existing EFTA research project. Fifteen analytical tools were built during the process — from a page verifier to a graph-based network analysis to a system that clusters the shapes of absent information. This is what that process found.

## 2. The Financial Myth

The Southern District of New York, together with the FBI, reviewed financial records from every bank that held Epstein's accounts: JP Morgan, Deutsche Bank, and TD Bank. Their conclusion:

> "We have not identified any accounts that are consistent with Epstein handling or investing other people's money." (Source: EFTA02731148)

Every account was personal. Deutsche Bank's own analysis — described in the prosecution memo as "extremely thorough, including a 50-page summary" — found "no immediate indication of financial criminal activity" and "no signs that Epstein was trading on behalf of any clients in Deutsche accounts during the period when he was a client, between 2013 and 2019." (Source: EFTA02731148)

Where did his money come from? Wexner's attorneys told SDNY that Epstein "stole or otherwise misappropriated several hundred million dollars from Wexner. That misconduct, together with fees that Epstein paid himself for his services to Wexner, appears to account for virtually all of Epstein's wealth." (Source: EFTA02731146)

On the same page as the no-client-accounts finding, the memo states: "contrary to some news reports, these searches did not reveal any cameras in any of the bedrooms or massage rooms." (Source: EFTA02731148)

Two dominant public narratives — hedge fund manager running a blackmail operation — are both unsupported by SDNY's own evidence. This specific finding does not appear in any other EFTA analysis project, including rhowardstone's 165-report dataset.

**Caveat:** "No immediate indication" is not "no evidence." The JP Morgan review was still in progress. Offshore accounts were not discussed. SDNY hedges: "appears to account for virtually all" is cautious language. The Wexner explanation came from Wexner's attorneys, not sworn testimony.

## 3. The Investigation That Produced Nothing

A systematic search of the full-text corpus identified **78 proffer agreements** — formal legal documents in which individuals agreed to provide information to the government under limited-use protections. These span April 2019 through July 2025.

Seven profferors are named: Leslie Groff (July 23, 2021), both Epstein pilots Lawrence Visoski and David Rodgers, Richard Barnett, Prince Andrew (date redacted), and Ghislaine Maxwell (July 24-25, 2025 — publicly released at [justice.gov/maxwell-interview](https://www.justice.gov/maxwell-interview)). Seventy-one profferors are redacted.

The timeline reveals an investigation that was far more active than the public record suggests:

- **Pre-arrest (April-May 2019):** 6 proffers — victims building the case
- **Post-arrest wave (July-September 2019):** ~25 proffers in 7 weeks, continuing uninterrupted after Epstein's death on August 10
- **Investigation phase (2020-2021):** Pilots proffered. Groff proffered 21 months after initially refusing.
- **Maxwell (2025):** Two-day proffer with Deputy Attorney General Todd Blanche in Tallahassee

One person was indicted: Ghislaine Maxwell. Plea negotiations with Sarah Kellen collapsed. Everyone else who proffered was never charged. No other EFTA analysis project has mapped the complete proffer landscape or produced this count.

## 4. Leslie Groff: The Largest Gap

Leslie Groff worked as Epstein's primary New York assistant from 2001 through the summer of 2019. She is named by virtually every victim in the prosecution memo as the person who scheduled their massages. She appears on 150,000+ pages of the EFTA files — second only to Epstein himself.

SDNY describes her role with uniquely minimizing language: "limited interactions with the victims beyond scheduling" and "spent little time in Epstein's residence." These phrases appear on only 3 of 2.89 million pages in the corpus — all in prosecution memos written by the same office. (Source: EFTA02731146)

Every victim contradicts this framing. One equates Groff with Maxwell and Epstein as people who "ruined her life." (Source: EFTA02731117) Another describes Groff as "right outside the closed door" during abuse. (Source: EFTA02731108)

Her payments were routed through the same trust infrastructure — Butterfly Trust and Southern Trust — used for other co-conspirator payments. Corpus emails show her directly scheduling massages: "Jeffrey is back in PB and asking if you might be available for a massage later this afternoon." (Source: EFTA01940212, DS10)

The prosecution memo, written October 2019, reports that Groff's attorney refused a proffer and invoked the Fifth Amendment. (Source: EFTA02731146) But a proffer agreement discovered in the corpus — **EFTA01682023** — shows she proffered with SDNY on July 23, 2021, under Acting U.S. Attorney Audrey Strauss. Her attorney, Michael Bachner, has publicly confirmed she "voluntarily spoke with prosecutors" and "was told that she was not being prosecuted."

The proffer agreement document itself — with its date, terms, and participants — does not appear to be cited by any other EFTA analysis project or public source. The fact of her cooperation is publicly known; the formal document is not.

A linguistic analysis of the prosecution memo reveals a consistent pattern: SDNY uses hedging language for people it ultimately didn't charge and confident language for people it did. Groff's hedging ratio (0.25) places her firmly in the non-charging zone. The memo's word choices, written before the final decisions, predicted the outcomes.

**The Kellen comparison:** Sarah Kellen performed a comparable scheduling role. She had NPA protection (Groff did not). SDNY considered charging Kellen — an AUSA email states she was "the only other individual we currently are considering charging" besides Maxwell. (Source: EFTA00106062) Plea negotiations were drafted but collapsed. Neither was ultimately charged. But Groff had more documented evidence (53 memo mentions vs. 0 for Kellen in the same memo), no NPA immunity, and no charging consideration documented.

## 5. The Excised Pages

Pages 68 through 85 of the prosecution memo (EFTA02731082) contain only the classified header. No content. This is not redaction — black bars over text — but excision: the content was removed before the document was released.

The structural skeleton survives. The legal framework on pages 67-74 is fully visible: three federal statutes analyzed (sex trafficking of minors, transportation of minors, enticement of minors), their elements listed, statute of limitations calculated. Then:

**Page 74:** "we evaluate whether there is sufficient evidence to charge" —

**Page 75:** "Leslie Groff **and/or** Ghislaine Maxwell with a federal crime and, if so, whether such charges would be in the interests of justice." (Source: EFTA02731157)

This is followed by five sections:
- **Section A** (pages 75-82, 8 pages): "[Redacted] — Evidence That [Redacted] Conspired to Sex Traffic Minors" with subsections "Knowledge of Sex Acts" and "Knowledge of Age." The longest and most detailed charging analysis — identity excised.
- **Section B** (pages 83-84): Name fully excised.
- **Section C**: No heading visible — either fully excised or absorbed.
- **Section D** (page 85): "Leslie Groff" — heading only, no content.
- **Section E** (page 85): "Ghislaine Maxwell" — released as a separate 32-page PDF (EFTA02731168).

Rep. Nancy Mace demanded the unredacted version of this exact memo on February 16, 2026. The DOJ told her SDNY applied the redactions before transmission — the department claims not to have the unredacted version. Rep. Laura Friedman found "hundreds" of pages still redacted despite the "unredacted" label.

This structural mapping of the excised pages has not been published elsewhere.

**Footnote 61** (page 67) reveals that JP Morgan produced Staley-Epstein messages to SDNY that temporally corroborate a victim's account of being raped by Jes Staley at Epstein's New York residence. SDNY had both victim testimony and financial institution records for this allegation. (Source: EFTA02731149)

## 6. The Network Structure

A graph analysis of 22 persons using fresh co-occurrence data from the corpus (231 pairs, 222 with non-zero co-occurrence) reveals two natural communities:

**Community 0 — Victim/Associate Cluster:** Maxwell, Dershowitz, Clinton, Copperfield, Dubin, Weinstein, Wexner, Prince Andrew, Kellen, Emmy Tayler. These are people named by victims or connected through testimony.

**Community 1 — Operations Cluster:** Groff, Kahn, Indyke, Visoski, Black, Rodgers, Mitchell, Barnett. These are people who ran the infrastructure — scheduling, accounting, legal, logistics.

SDNY charged from Community 0 (Maxwell). Community 1 — the operational backbone — was never touched. The densest connections in the entire network are Indyke-Kahn (8,607 shared pages), Groff-Kahn (7,835), Groff-Indyke (5,228), and Groff-Visoski (4,761). These four form the operational core.

Emmy Tayler — Maxwell's former assistant from 1997, described by an AUSA as "potentially the most critical person in the case" (EFTA00106062) — has the highest betweenness centrality: she is the structural bridge connecting all communities. The FBI planned a London interview for March 19, 2020; COVID lockdowns cancelled it. No proffer agreement exists for her. Her story is publicly known; her structural centrality in the network is our quantification.

## 7. What Can and Cannot Be Known

73 specific questions were extracted from the files themselves. 38% are answered or partially answered. Seven walls define the hard limits of what this dataset can reveal:

1. **Section D content** — Groff's charging analysis, excised from the release
2. **Section A identity** — 8-page analysis for an unnamed person, excised
3. **Proffer content** — what Groff, Visoski, Andrew told SDNY is legally protected
4. **18 excised pages** — the entire charging section stripped from the document
5. **AUSA names** — redacted throughout, preventing author attribution
6. **71 redacted profferors** — identity and content both unknown
7. **Offshore accounts** — SDNY's memo discusses three banks; USVI banking not mentioned

These walls require action outside the dataset: FOIA requests for the unredacted prosecution memo, congressional subpoenas for Section D content, or DOJ Inspector General review of the release process.

## 9. Methodology

**Processing:** 4,251 PDFs OCR-processed with Tesseract 4.1.1 (200 DPI, English). 18 text files, ~6.8 MB total. Off-by-one OCR marker correction discovered and documented: Tesseract captures page footers AFTER body text, so the EFTA marker below a passage is its correct page number.

**Verification:** Every EFTA citation verified by rendering the original PDF page and comparing to OCR text. 56/56 citations independently verified against rhowardstone's corpus (different extraction pipeline, same underlying PDFs).

**Analysis tools built:** EFTA Verifier (PDF page rendering), entity network (SpaCy NER with 6 noise filters), QA check (automated repo validation), lead finder (systematic gap identification), financial evidence extraction, redaction analysis, phone pattern analysis, unified knowledge base (22 persons), graph analysis (networkx: betweenness, eigenvector, PageRank, community detection), hole geometry (cosine similarity clustering of absent information), triangulation (cross-question inference chains), temporal proximity analysis, redaction bridging, payment-event correlation, co-absence analysis, linguistic pattern analysis, solve map (73 questions scored), and automated rebuild script.

**AI disclosure:** All analysis text was generated by Claude (Anthropic). Direction, verification, source selection, and editorial decisions were made by Adrian Moen. Every finding was extracted from the documents first and written second — no claims from memory. Community corrections were integrated (Dubin victim identity ambiguity, SDNY corroboration caveat for "lent out" accounts).

## 10. Limitations

1. **The prosecution memo is not the full investigation.** It is one document from one point in time (December 2019). The investigation continued for years afterward.
2. **Proffer count includes potential duplicates.** The same agreement filed in multiple datasets produces multiple hits. 78 total likely represents 40-50 unique sessions.
3. **Linguistic analysis identifies patterns, not intent.** Hedging language correlates with non-charging but does not prove the decisions were made for improper reasons.
4. **Graph metrics depend on LIKE queries** against the corpus, which may include false positives (common last names like "Mitchell" matching non-Epstein contexts).
5. **Section A identity is contested.** Two hypotheses (person-specific vs. charge-specific organization) are both consistent with the surviving headings. Neither is provable without the excised content.
6. **"Genuinely new" is verified as of March 22, 2026.** Independent researchers may have published the same findings without our knowledge.
7. **This analysis documents the gap between evidence and prosecution.** It cannot determine WHY only Maxwell was charged. The gap is a fact. The cause is behind the walls.
