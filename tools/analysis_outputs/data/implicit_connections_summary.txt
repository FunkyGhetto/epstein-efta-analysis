IMPLICIT CONNECTIONS SUMMARY
============================
Five analysis methods applied to prosecution memos + 2.89M-page corpus.

METHOD 1 — TEMPORAL PROXIMITY
  33 specific dates extracted, 55 significant cross-document matches.
  KEY: July 2, 2019 (indictment day) — Groff emailed Rich Kahn at 12:39 AM.
  She was active hours before the indictment was unsealed.
  STRENGTH: Medium. Shows Groff's operational role but doesn't prove
  she knew about the indictment.

METHOD 2 — REDACTION BRIDGING
  102 bridges across 10 event signatures.
  KEY: FBI 302s exist for the "coffee shop" victim (EFTA00086883), the
  "massage school" victim (EFTA01246406), and the "lent out" victim
  (EFTA01249021). These FBI interview records may contain fewer
  redactions than the prosecution memo.
  KEY: SDNY internal review (EFTA01649143) describes the "lent out"
  testimony as "the closest account" — suggesting they considered it
  the strongest evidence of third-party abuse but still could not
  corroborate it.
  STRENGTH: High. The FBI 302 bridge documents are the most actionable
  finding — they are the primary source records for the victim testimony
  summarized in the prosecution memo.

METHOD 3 — PAYMENT-EVENT CORRELATION
  14 cross-document financial matches.
  KEY: Groff's $100K wires (Jan 2017, Dec 2017) routed through Butterfly
  Trust and Southern Trust — the SAME trust infrastructure used for
  Marcinkova's $100K and the redacted $250K co-conspirator payment
  after the Miami Herald. This puts Groff in the same financial
  architecture as the known co-conspirators.
  STRENGTH: High. Documented bank records and wire memoranda.

METHOD 4 — CO-ABSENCE ANALYSIS
  6 people with HIGH corpus presence but ZERO memo pages:
  - Larry Visoski (pilot): 29,993 corpus pages
  - Donald Trump: 16,886 corpus pages
  - George Mitchell: 13,885 corpus pages
  - Ehud Barak: 7,654 corpus pages
  - Bill Richardson: 3,132 corpus pages
  - Sarah Kellen: 1,500 corpus pages

  These are people the documents discuss extensively but the prosecution
  memo avoids entirely. The most striking is Visoski — the chief pilot
  who flew every documented flight — never appearing in the prosecution
  analysis of the trafficking operation that depended on those flights.
  STRENGTH: Medium-High. The pattern is real, but absence from one memo
  doesn't mean absence from investigation. Some may appear in other
  prosecution documents we haven't analyzed.

METHOD 5 — LINGUISTIC PATTERN ANALYSIS
  People SDNY ultimately charged get confident language.
  People never charged get hedging language.

  Charged (strong): Maxwell (50%), Andrew (100%), Brunel (100%)
  Never charged (hedged): Wexner (0%), Black (28%), Groff (25%), Indyke (0%)

  The most significant: Wexner's wealth explanation — "appears to account
  for virtually all of Epstein's wealth" — uses hedging language. SDNY
  presents its own theory about where the money came from with caution,
  not certainty.
  STRENGTH: Medium. Linguistic patterns correlate with charging decisions
  but don't prove causation. Prosecutors may hedge for legal reasons
  even when privately confident.

================================================================================
COMBINED ASSESSMENT: WHAT THE IMPLICIT METHODS FOUND THAT EXPLICIT DIDN'T
================================================================================

1. GROFF'S FINANCIAL INFRASTRUCTURE CONNECTION (Methods 1+3)
   The explicit evidence shows Groff scheduling massages. The implicit
   evidence shows her payments routed through the same trust entities as
   other co-conspirator payments. Combined with her 12:39 AM email on
   indictment day, this paints a picture of someone far more integrated
   into the operation than the prosecution memo's "limited interactions"
   characterization suggests.

2. THE PROSECUTION MEMO AS A SELECTIVE NARRATIVE (Methods 4+5)
   The co-absence analysis shows the memo omits extensively documented
   people. The linguistic analysis shows the memo uses hedging for people
   it didn't charge. Together: the prosecution memo is not a comprehensive
   record of what SDNY knew. It is a selective narrative that avoids
   certain names and hedges on others.

3. THE FBI 302 BRIDGE DOCUMENTS (Method 2)
   The most actionable implicit finding: FBI interview records exist for
   multiple victims whose testimony is summarized (with heavy redaction)
   in the prosecution memo. These 302s may contain fewer redactions and
   more detail. They are identified by specific EFTA numbers and could
   be requested or FOIA'd individually.

4. THE WEXNER WEALTH THEORY IS HEDGED (Method 5)
   SDNY presents "Epstein's wealth came from Wexner" as their theory,
   but uses cautious language throughout. Combined with the "no client
   accounts" finding: SDNY found no evidence of OTHER money sources
   but also doesn't state with confidence that Wexner was the ONLY
   source. The gap between "we haven't found other sources" and "there
   are no other sources" is significant.
